What is one meant to do with their life?

I wonder why I do things. Why I work, why I go to school, why I go to the gym. Why do I do anything?

Gym to have a better body so that I can be happier with myself and look good. Work and school so I make money and buy nice things. Why do I want nice things, why do I want to look nicem, why do I want to be happy? Isn't it all just chemical reactions happening in my brain? Is that the purpose of life? To satisfy the desires subjected upon me by my brain’s baser instincts.

I think if you recursively ask someone why they are doing something, you always hit the impenetrable wall of “I do ‘x’ because it satisfies some urge in my brain”. This claim can probably be disproven but I'm yet to think of a good counterexample.

Kinda sounds like everything our person here is doing is motivated by some deeply ingrained desire in their brain. Desires that probably formed over millions of years of evolution. If satisfying those desires is the meaning of life, then life’s grim. That can’t be all there is to it.

I once heard on Lex Fridman’s podcast that “Life is the universe understanding itself”. I really like that. What’s the purpose of life? Maybe it’s to answer that very question.

If you are anything but an atheist, then you must believe in god, reincarnation, spirits or something like that. I believe in God. So maybe the meaning of life is to live the way your religion instructs you to. But there’s so many religions, and they conflict with one another. They can’t all be right. Maybe they can, but my limited monkey brain’s logic is pretty sure that when two statements conflict, they can’t both be right. By that logic, not all religions can be right. Some must be false. Which ones are those? Which ones are correct? Are any of them correct?

If you are an atheist. Then you probably believe we are a product of evolution. Why does evolution happen? Why does the engine of life claw and fight as much as it can to sustain itself? I’ve seen entropy as a proposed answer. Life maximizes entropy*. And the universe wants to maximize entropy.

I’m not qualified to reason through these big ideas of religion, evolution and entropy, I need to do some homework.

My homework wouldn’t make much of a difference though. I don’t think there’s any text, be it religious or scientific in nature, that can explain why the universe exists in the first place.

Science says Big Bang. WTF is that? We have no idea why the Big Bang happened and what came before it.

Religions often say God created the universe. So where does God come from? “We don’t know, and we don’t ask. Even if we were given the answer we wouldn’t understand it.” Fair enough. Maybe our simple tools of logic and linear perception of time cannot make sense of the answers to our own questions.

Regardless of what ideology I flee to in search of meaning, I hit a wall of unanswered and seemingly unanswerable questions. So for the time being, maybe that’s it. That’s the purpose of life. Answer these questions. Make some goddamn sense of it all. The purpose of life is to determine the purpose of life.

One human in one measly lifetime cannot make much progress in this grand mission. However, generations of knowledge passed down can. So I think that’s it, play your part in advancing humanity to be sufficiently technologically and ideologically advanced to contend with these questions, and you’ve lived a purposeful life.

That’s the meaning of my life. I want to work hard to advance humanity, whether that be by making better AI, or educating the next generation, or serving pizzas at a restaurant, I want to contribute meaningfully and to the best of my ability. Hopefully I can do more for the world than serve pizza. Along the way, I’ll satisfy plenty of my seemingly pointless inner desires so that I can have what feels like a great time.

Signing off now. Bye bye.
